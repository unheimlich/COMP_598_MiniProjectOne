Newcastle named the UK's best airport for the third year in succession... with London Stansted and Luton finishing joint bottom

  • Newcastle scores highly on queuing, bag drop and passport control
  • Heathrow Terminal 5 comes second, with Birmingham in third place
  • London Southend takes crown of best small airport in the UK 
  • The accolades come from a survey of over 10,000 Which? members  

By John Hutchinson for MailOnline

Published: 15:42 GMT, 24 September 2015 | Updated: 09:31 GMT, 25 September 2015

160 shares

91

comments

Newcastle Airport has been named the UK's best large airport for the third year running by influential consumer group Which?

London Stansted and London Luton finished joint bottom of the pile of those surveyed in the large airports category.

Passengers were asked to rate the airports on categories including the check-in experience, quality of food outlets and seating for Which? Travel magazine. Newcastle scored particularly highly on queues at security, bag drop and passport control.

Newcastle Airport was named the best UK large airport in the Which? survey of over 11,200 people

Newcastle scored particularly highly on queues at security, bag drop and passport control 

The final standings were revealed after 11,266 Which? members shared their experiences of the UK's airports with the group's magazine. Newcastle, which is used by four million passengers annually, received a customer satisfaction score of 72 per cent.

In second spot was London Heathrow Terminal 5 with 66 per cent, then Birmingham with 65 per cent.

London Stansted and Luton Airport both scored 40 per cent, with customers complaining about long queues and inadequate seating.

Belfast International was positioned just above them with a score of 51 per cent - the quality of food outlets being the biggest concern.

Long queues and inadequate seating were the main gripes with customers at London Stansted

HOW THE AIRPORTS RANKED IN THE WHICH? SURVEY

 

The best large airports

Newcastle International - 72%

Heathrow Terminal 5 - 66%

Birmingham International - 65%

 

The worst large airports

London Stansted - 40%

London Luton - 40%

Belfast International - 51%

The best small airports

London Southend - 86%

Robin Hood Doncaster Sheffield - 84%

London City - 81%

 

The worst small airports

Aberdeen International - 51%

Leeds Bradford International - 53%

Inverness - 57%

Percentages are the overall customer score based on a variety of categories. 

There was also an award for the best small airport, with London Southend taking that honour with an 86 per cent customer score.

The airport scored the full five-star rating on queues at baggage drop and security, as well as passport control and baggage control. The overall score was diminished by a two-star rating on the food outlets.

Robin Hood Doncaster Sheffield wasn't far behind with 84 per cent and London City touched down with 81 per cent. 

Customers seemed happy with the services received at London Southend in the Which? poll

Dave Laws, chief executive of Newcastle International Airport, told MailOnline Travel: 'Our aim is to be the most welcoming in the UK, so we are delighted to once again place top among the larger airports.

'This is the third year running that passengers have scored us higher than any other large airport. It's a testament to the work of our staff and business partners, who do all they can to make a passenger's journey through the airport enjoyable.

'The result is even more impressive as it came when we were undertaking £14 million development work to our departure lounge to further improve the customer experience. I'd like to thank our passengers for their patience during construction.'

'This has topped an exciting few weeks for the airport, as we have just announced the return of the United Airlines service to New York for summer 2016 as well as new flights to Barcelona with Spanish low cost carrier Vueling.'
